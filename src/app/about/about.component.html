About Me

Currently, I am a Developer Advocate for DataStax, the only active-everywhere database enterprise for cloud platforms. Solving unique problems, giving back to my community, and growing from the outcome of my work, are all reasons why I love doing what I do.

This passion is the compass for the roles and projects I take on, with a primary interest in promoting women in STEM. This has been expressed throughout my career in various forms, like becoming a full-stack teaching assistant for UNC-Charlotte's Coding Boot Camp, and former Chapter leader of the Charlotte Chapter of Girl Develop It (GDI).

This past January, my colleagues and I have started Queen City Bytes, an organization dedicated to supporting women by offering technical instruction, educational resources, and an environment where networking and mentorship are valued. Come be apart of our community.

Another initiative I am proud to be a leader of is Fleurix, Charlotte's first Women in Technology Conference. Fleurix aims to increase the number of women pursuing and thriving in technology-related careers by connecting women with an inspiring community and accessible resources that can help conquer common barriers to entry, retention, and promotion of women in IT.

And hey, maybe I'm biased, but the best tech community I've seen thus far is none other than the Charlotte Devs. Our mission is to grow Charlotte's tech community by bringing together developers of all skill levels and interests. We are not only focused on discussing and improving particular skillsets, but to improve the Charlotte Developer community as a whole through various interactions. One of those interactions includes Slack, an integrated chat application (like AIM for adults). Join our conversation by clicking this link.

Advocating for aspiring technologists is my passion and ingrained in my work. If you'd like to know more about the tech industry, tips on how to get started, or want to learn more about who I am, let's chat over a cup of coffee.
